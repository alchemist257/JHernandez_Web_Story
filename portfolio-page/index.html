Skills

As a student at the University of Utah, I studied geology. During this time, I had an internship at the Natural History Museum of Utah. Here I helped managed the Excel databases of more than 30,000 specimens. I learned proper lab procedure as a scientist, field testing, and critical thinking. I focused on environmental courses in school so my geologic knowledge pertains more to the earth as we know it know, instead of that of the past.

I decided to learn web development alongside geology because I liked the process of creation more than research in most cases. I started with some YouTube tutorials on building a simple HTML webpage and just went from there.
I am comfortable with HTML/CSS, CSS animations, JavaScript, Node, Express, mySQL, Bootstrap, SemanticUI, and Adobe Photoshop.

Experience

Before attending the University of Utah in 2013, I ventured off to Zhenjiang, China to teach English. I taught at a high school as well as a preschool. As you can imagine, each came with their own set of challenges. Above all else, I learned the value of communication and knowing your audience. Because I was in charge of all the course material, I got my first real taste of self-reliance in the workplace. Since then, every job I've worked has had elements that require me to do things on my own, and I've come to enjoy that.

As mentioned above, my main job in college was an internship at the Natural History Museum of Utah in the paleontology department. The museum provided a great amount of job duties. I cataloged micropaleontology specimens(teeth, claws, and vertebraes of very small creatures), educated the public during special events, rock-climbed to dig sites, and shuffled fossils in collection so they could be used by the exhibits.

During my first year in college, I donated plasma to pay the bills that the museum internship didn't cover. After that first year, I took a 2nd job as a tour guide for SLC Bike Taxi. This job did more for my people skills than all my life experience combined had before. I thrived as a salesman and tour guide, selling people on hour long rides around Salt Lake City, and then delivering a tour experience that lived up to how I presented it. This was my primary source of income for more than 2 years, and I still take some shifts when I have extra free time.

I got an entrepreneurial bug shortly after that job, and have opened 2 businesses since then. 1 is still in operation now. That business is a guesthouse located in downtown Salt Lake City. I found a lot of my clients on the bike taxi wanted somewhere to stay downtown that wasn't a hotel. I did some research on zoning laws and the basics of the hospitality industry. I rented a 3 bedroom apartment in downtown Salt Lake City and opened Tour Guide Guesthouse shortly after. Since 2016, I've hosted over 250 unique guests.
